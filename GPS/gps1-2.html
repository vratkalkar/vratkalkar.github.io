What Is Pair Programming?

Pair programming is technique where two developers work on a single task together, most commonly switching between the roles of driver and navigator. There are also other configurations such as ping pong.

Why is Pair Programming Important to DBC?

The aspect of teamwork to accomplish a goal, which aligns with a lot of the key tenets. It also helps both pairs to learn by sharing knowledge, picking up tricks from each other, and minimizing errors since there are two pairs of eyes.

Compare Driver and Navigator

The driver controls the keyboard, relying on the navigator as a guide while keeping the scope or big picture of the task in mind.
